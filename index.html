The problem

The Problem

Complex care cases remain clinically invisible despite comprehensive documentation.

Fragmented Medical Histories

Complex chronic care generates documentation across multiple systems, specialists, and time periods. Each consultation operates from fragments — incomplete views of the patient's clinical journey.

The Missing Continuity Layer

In complex cases, the person carrying the case often becomes the only continuity layer across time, systems, and clinical decisions. The clinically readable whole-case orientation doesn't emerge from the records alone.

Missed Patterns

When each consultation starts from fragments, critical patterns and longitudinal context remain obscured, affecting clinical decision-making and care coordination.

One reconstructed trajectory — findings, open questions, hypotheses and a documentation gap, on one axis.

Validation

Validation Thesis

What we aim to validate with clinical partners.

These research hypotheses guide our partnership conversations with clinical validation partners.

H1

Longitudinal orientation improves care coordination

Clinician teams with access to source-referenced, longitudinal case views demonstrate improved coordination and fewer redundant diagnostic efforts.

H2

Pattern recognition accelerates insight

Clinical teams identify meaningful patterns more quickly when the longitudinal case is presented coherently rather than as fragmented records.

H3

Time investment decreases for case orientation

Clinicians spend less time on case orientation and re-reading fragmented records, gaining time for clinical reasoning and decision-making.

H4

Clinical credibility depends on source transparency

Source-referenced orientation is essential for clinical trust and appropriate decision-making in complex care settings.

Founder

The Founder

NAVORA's story and founding perspective.

Built from the vantage point where the whole case became visible. NAVORA was founded by Nilab Sekandari, shaped by lived experience with complex chronic care, caregiver responsibility, and professional work in regulated, documentation-heavy environments. The founding insight is simple: complex cases can be fully documented and still not be clinically readable as a whole. When each consultation starts from fragments, the person carrying the case often becomes the only continuity layer across time, systems, and decisions.

"I didn't see the pattern because I knew more medicine. I saw it because I was holding the whole timeline."

— Nilab Sekandari, Founder

NAVORA exists to make that whole-case coherence visible and accessible to clinical teams, without requiring one person to hold the entire timeline.
