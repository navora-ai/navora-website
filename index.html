What NAVORA does

NAVORA does not count labels.
It reconstructs trajectories.

Longitudinal-first

Reconstructs what happened across time — not only what one encounter documented.

NAVORA assembles years of scattered records into a single readable trajectory: what happened, in what order, with which turning points and gaps.

Source-traceable

Every relevant statement stays linked to where it came from.

Document, date, section, finding, observation or hypothesis — each statement is designed to remain traceable to its origin and review status. Trust requires source referencing.

Gap-aware

Shows not only what is known — but what is missing or contradictory.

NAVORA helps make missing information, conflicting statements and unresolved clinical questions visible, so they can be reviewed rather than overlooked.

Human-controlled

The judgement stays with the clinician

NAVORA supports orientation. It makes no autonomous diagnosis or treatment decision.

Useful early

Works before full interoperability

NAVORA starts from existing PDFs and reports, and gets stronger as interoperability improves.

How it works

Structured clinical objects —
not a text summary.

A high-level view. Every step stays reviewable and traceable, with human oversight throughout.

Step 01

Ingestion

Existing reports, documents and observations enter one structured case context.

Step 02

Structuring & differentiation

Information becomes structured objects — distinguishing findings, observations, diagnoses and hypotheses.

Step 03

Timeline reconstruction

The course of the case is reconstructed over time, making turning points and gaps visible.

Step 04

Source referencing

Each relevant statement stays linked to its original source, date and context.

Step 05

Gap & contradiction detection

Missing information, conflicts and open questions are surfaced for review.

Step 06

Briefing & human review

A reviewable working view that a clinician verifies — never an automated conclusion.

Modular vision

From clinical context to system navigation.

The foundation — current MVP focus and near-term validation.

NAVORA Core

Core · MVP

The structured foundation for data logic, privacy principles and source traceability. Core distinguishes findings, observations, diagnoses and hypotheses — reviewable rather than merely generative.

NAVORA Klio

MVP-near

The clinical story engine. Turns scattered records into a readable case history — what happened, what is known, what stays open. For first consultations, second opinions and case conferences.

NAVORA Tractus

MVP-near

The longitudinal pathway layer. Makes the course of illness visible over time — turning points, deteriorations, gaps and possible triggers. It does not diagnose.

Validation path — strengthening reviewable insight over time.

Care / Continuum

Validation path

The bridge between appointments and daily life. Helps capture relapses, stabilisation, limits and warning signals between encounters — trajectory awareness, not isolated snapshots.

Pattern Intelligence

Validation path

A layer for surfacing recurring, reviewable patterns across symptoms, labs, medication, sleep or exertion. It does not diagnose — it makes patterns visible for human review.

Cohort Intelligence

Long-term vision

A future anonymised learning layer comparing similar complex trajectories. For research, quality improvement and system learning — once ethically, legally and technically governed.

Long-term platform vision — reducing coordination burden.

Patient Companion

NAVORA Nexus

Initial focus

Where the gap is clearest.

NAVORA begins with complex chronic trajectories — years of fragmentation, repeated re-explanation, many specialties, unclear responsibilities, unresolved questions.

ME / CFSLong CovidDysautonomia / POTSMultisystem chronic care

These cases make the structural gap especially visible. The same case logic is designed to extend later to other complex care journeys — once validated where the need is most acute.

Trust & governance

Source referencing is the basis of trust.

Source traceability

Human-in-the-loop

Consent & legal basis

Regulatory pathway under review
